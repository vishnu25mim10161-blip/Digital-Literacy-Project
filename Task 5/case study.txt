Case Study: Cyberbullying  

Cyberbullying involves the use of digital platforms to harass, threaten, or humiliate an 
individual. Unlike traditional bullying, it is persistent, reaches a vast audience instantly, and 
often leaves a permanent digital footprint. This case study examines a realistic scenario 
involving a college student targeted by "Deepfake" technology. 
How it Happens : 
1. Selection: A student is targeted following a minor campus dispute or simply 
because their social media profile is public and contains high-quality photos. 
2. Creation: The bully uses easily accessible AI tools to "morph" the victim's face onto 
suggestive or compromising images/videos, creating a highly realistic but entirely 
fake "Deepfake." 
3. Dissemination: The attacker shares this media across anonymous college 
confession pages and private WhatsApp groups, often using a "burner" (disposable) 
account to hide their identity. 
4. The Pressure: The bully then contacts the victim directly, threatening to send the 
media to their parents or the college administration unless a "ransom" is paid or the 
victim deletes their social media. 
5. The Impact: The victim, fearing social ruin, experiences severe psychological 
distress, isolation, and academic decline. 
Typical Targets: While anyone can be a victim, female college students are 
disproportionately targeted for image-based abuse. High-profile students, such as student 
union leaders or campus influencers, are also frequently targeted to damage their 
reputations. 
Consequences: The consequences are grave, ranging from severe anxiety and depression 
to academic expulsion or self-harm. Legally, the perpetrator can be charged under 
Sections 66E (Privacy Violation) and 67 (Publishing Obscene Material) of the IT Act, 
leading to imprisonment and a permanent criminal record.
